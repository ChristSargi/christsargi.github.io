The Pentagon Network

The network is divided into five wings, each representing a wall of the Pentagon. Each wing has a switch configured with VLANs for different departments: VLAN 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, and 60 correspond to the Navy, Air Force, Army, NSA, DIA, and Guests, respectively. I configured inter-VLAN routing to enable communication between VLANs 40 and 50 while isolating them from other VLANs. Each wing’s router ensures data transfer between these two VLANs. A central switch connects the VLANs from all wings and links them to a control room, where all VLANs (except Guests) can communicate. To connect the Pentagon to the White House, I used WAN networking with static routes and implemented a backup route for fault tolerance in case of hardware or connection failures. The main objectives of this project were to

Build a simulation of the Pentagon’s computer network.
Configure VLANs for the different departments available in the building.
Ensure successful inter-VLAN routing between specified departments.
Utilize a WAN connection between the White House and the Pentagon while maintaining fault tolerance.
Ensure secure communication between devices on the network and remote management on intermediary devices.
